Valerie Nelson1 grew up in West Palm Beach, Florida, where she became quite fond of the colourful architecture and tropical plants2 that are often seen as cliche and tacky. As a child, she would hide in corners and formulate stories and ideas on paper3 that she had been unable to verbally express. She would attend small drawing5 classes and pottery classes at a nearby art center, still seldom speaking out loud. Her favourite activities as a child were drawing while the radio4 played, intently watching nickelodeon, and swinging on palm fronds.

Valerie had lived her childhood6 in a sort of daze, primarily day-dreaming, and not being entirely self-aware. In 2008, Valerie was accepted into A.W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts7. After a few months, she became comfortable there, and had realized many other students were similar to her. There, she was able to paint8 and animate9 the ideas and stories she wanted to tell, this time in a more refined and detailed manner than before. There she also grew a large interest in science fiction10, pop music, and dogs, all philosophically and aesthetically.

After an aeon11 of thinking and weighing certain pros and cons, Valerie made the choice to attend Rhode Island School of Design12, with no solid ideas of what she wanted to do in life, only knowing that she enjoyed—and was apparently good at—painting and illustrating stories12. With even further consideration, Valerie went into the Graphic Design major, mostly because it was a field that she had no prior knowledge about yet fascinated her. She likes how Graphic Design13 is a broad term that has multiple meanings today, and can allow for the creative visual input and storytelling in multiple platforms. She also likes how its purpose is to speak to people clearly and effectively14, something Valerie has always strived for but found difficult. There is also an emphasis on colour and composition in Graphic Design, two aspects of visual storytelling15 Valerie had always emphasized in her work. Here, now currently in this major, she has been creating works that revolve around her differing interests, from pop stars16, to video games17, to the theory of touch18.

In addition to schooling, Valerie currently works as an Image Technician at the the RISD Museum19, paints commissioned pet portraits20, and just finished up a summer Design Internship at Concrete21. In the future, Valerie hopes to get involved with layout design, brand design, and art direction as much as animating, film-making, and becoming a pop star.
