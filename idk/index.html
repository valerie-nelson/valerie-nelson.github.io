Valerie Nelson1 grew up in West Palm Beach, Florida, where she became quite fond of the colorful architecture and tropical plants2 that are often seen as cliche and tacky. As a child she would hide in corners and formulate stories and ideas on paper3 that she had been unable to verbally express. She would attend small drawing classes and pottery classes at a nearby art center, still seldom speaking out loud. Her favorite activities as a child were drawing while the radio4 played, intently watching nickelodeon, and swinging on palm fronds.

Valerie had lived her childhood5 in a sort of daze, primarily day dreaming, and not being entirely self-aware. In 2008, Valerie was accepted into A.W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts6. After a few months, she became comfortable there, and had realized many other students were similar to her. There, she was able to paint7 and animate8 the ideas and stories she wanted to tell, this time in a more refined and detailed manner than before. She also grew a large interest in science fiction9 at that time, both philosophically and aesthetically.

After an aeon10 of thinking and weighing certain pros and cons, Valerie made the choice to attend Rhode Island School of Design11, with no solid ideas of what she wanted to do in life, only knowing that she enjoyed—and was apparently good at—painting and illustrating stories12. With even further consideration, Valerie went into the Graphic Design major, mostly because it was a field that she had no prior knowledge about yet fascinated her. She likes how Graphic Design12 is a broad term that could mean many different things and can allow for the creative visual input and storytelling in multiple platforms. She also likes how it can speak to people clearly and effectively, something Valerie has always strived for but found difficult. There is also an emphasis on color and composition in Graphic Design, two aspects of visual storytelling13 Valerie had always emphasized in her work. Here, now currently in this major, she has been creating works that revolve around her differing interests, from pop stars14, to video games15, to the theory of touch16.

Valerie also currently works at the the RISD Museum17, paints commissioned pet portraits18, and just finished up a Design Internship at Concrete19. In the future, Valerie hopes to get involved with layout design, brand design, and art direction as much as animating, film-making, and becoming a pop star.
